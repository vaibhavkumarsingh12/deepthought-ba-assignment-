# Question 1 — Sourcing Methods for the Federer Profile

*Vaibhav Kumar Singh — DeepThought BA Assignment Part B*

---

## The framing principle

A standard Google search returns what companies *say about themselves*. The Federer profile is identified by what companies *spend money on* — land they acquire, technical talent they hire, and capital equipment they import. The 12 methods below are organized along this triangulation logic: **Land** (physical expansion signals), **Labor** (technical hiring signals), and **Capital** (machinery, IP, and certification investment). Most candidate sourcing lists rely on the first 3–4 of these methods; the remainder are where the Federer profile actually surfaces.

---

## Tier 1 — Foundational sources (use this in this project)

**1. DSIR R&D Recognition Registry** (`dsir.gov.in`)
The Department of Scientific & Industrial Research maintains a public list of all in-house R&D units recognized by the Government of India. Filterable by city and segment. Strong filter for *differentiated manufacturers* — recognition requires substantive R&D infrastructure, not branded marketing.
*Limitation:* Skews toward older, formally structured companies. Newer specialty players (founded post-2018) may not yet be DSIR-recognized.

**2. BSE SME and NSE Emerge listing platforms**
Sub-Rs.500Cr listed companies with verifiable revenue, promoter shareholding, and full DRHP/RHP filings. The DRHP itself is a primary-source treasure trove — facility addresses, promoter backgrounds, growth plans, risk factors.
*Limitation:* Captures only the subset that has chosen to list. Misses unlisted family-owned manufacturers.

**3. MCA filings via Tofler / Zauba Corp / Probe42**
Direct access to RoC-filed financials for any registered Indian company. Verifies revenue band, paid-up capital, director list with appointment dates (critical for ownership/PE check). Free tier sufficient for individual lookups.
*Limitation:* Annual filings lag by 6–12 months. Some private companies file delayed. Multi-entity structures can hide true scale.

**4. State industrial directories — MIDC / GIDC / KIADB / TIDCO**
Captures the unlisted manufacturing universe organized by physical industrial cluster (MIDC Bhosari, GIDC Ankleshwar, etc.). Useful for finding founder-driven operations missed by DSIR.
*Limitation:* Frequently outdated. Smaller MIDC plots may not be listed in published directories.

---

## Tier 2 — Land signals (physical expansion = growth proof)

**5. State Industrial Corporation land-allotment notifications**
MIDC, GIDC, KIADB, and similar bodies publish periodic land-allotment lists in state gazettes and on their portals. A company being allotted land in a *Mega Industrial Park* — particularly clusters earmarked for medical devices, defence, or specialty chemicals — is by definition in growth mode and pre-classified as a specialty player by the state itself.
*Why this is high-signal:* Land allotment requires capital commitment and 18–36 month construction visibility. Companies don't acquire industrial plots speculatively. Listing in a thematic cluster (e.g. AMTZ Visakhapatnam Medical Devices Park) auto-confirms segment fit.
*Limitation:* Allotment lists are scattered across state-level publications; aggregation requires manual cross-referencing. Some allotments are for warehousing rather than manufacturing — verify intent.

**6. PLI Scheme awardee lists**
Production-Linked Incentive scheme awardees are published per-sector (specialty chemicals, medical devices, electronics, white goods). Every awardee has a public-domain capacity-expansion plan, investment commitment number, and sector classification.
*Why this is high-signal:* PLI awardee status confirms specialty-manufacturer classification by Government of India *and* multi-year growth commitment. Combines C3 (differentiation), C5 (tailwind), and C6 (growth) in one signal.
*Limitation:* Skews to mid-large companies (most PLI schemes have minimum investment thresholds of Rs.50–250Cr). Misses sub-Rs.50Cr specialty players.

---

## Tier 3 — Capital signals (where the money actually goes)

**7. Patent filings on the Indian Patent Office database** (`ipindia.gov.in`)
Recent filings by sub-Rs.500Cr Indian-applicant manufacturers indicate active intellectual-property building — what your notes correctly call "intellectual muscles." Search filterable by applicant address (city), date range, and IPC classification.
*Why this is high-signal:* Patent prosecution is expensive (Rs.4–10 lakh per filing including agent costs) and meaningless without commercialisation intent. A manufacturer filing 3+ patents in the last 36 months is investing in differentiation, by revealed preference.
*Limitation:* High false-positive rate (many filings are defensive or never commercialised). Requires manual filtering for substantive specialty-relevant patents vs. trivial process-improvement filings.

**8. USFDA / EU-GMP / WHO-PQ / CDSCO certified-facility databases**
Authoritative regulatory listings of Indian manufacturing facilities with international quality approvals. USFDA Establishment Inspection Reports are searchable by Indian state. EU-GMP certifications are indexed in the EUDRA-GMDP database. WHO-PQ has a public list. CDSCO maintains state-wise IVD/medical-device manufacturer lists.
*Why this is high-signal:* International regulatory certification is the most expensive credential a specialty manufacturer can hold (Rs.1–3 Cr per audit cycle plus ongoing compliance cost). Holding one is *prima facie* evidence of specialty-grade capability.
*Limitation:* Domain-restricted to pharma, biotech, medical devices, and food. Not relevant for industrial sensors, performance chemicals, or non-regulated specialty segments.

**9. Equipment-vendor case studies and reference-customer mining**
The supply-chain inverse search from your notes — refined. High-end equipment vendors (Agilent, Waters, Bruker, Thermo Fisher, Haas, DMG Mori) publish customer case studies, "trusted by" reference logos, and channel-partner installation announcements. Indian dealers of these brands frequently announce wins on LinkedIn.
*Why this is high-signal:* If a manufacturer has invested Rs.50L–5Cr in an HPLC-MS, NMR, or 5-axis CNC, they are by revealed preference operating at specialty grade. Vendors are motivated to publicize installations because case studies drive their next sale.
*Limitation:* Vendors don't publish full client lists. This method requires manually mining ~15–20 separate vendor websites and dealer LinkedIn pages. Lower yield, higher precision.

## Tier 4 — Labor signals (hiring is the leading indicator)

**10. LinkedIn Sales Navigator + Naukri job-posting density**
Filter LinkedIn by company size + headquarters city + technical job-titles ("Process Chemist", "QA Manager", "R&D Lead", "Head of Operational Excellence", "Production Engineer"). Hiring activity itself is C6 evidence — companies don't hire technical leadership unless they're investing in capability.
*Why this is high-signal:* Hiring decisions lead financial reporting by 6–18 months. A specialty manufacturer hiring an R&D Lead in October is preparing for capability that shows up in next year's financials. Spotting this in October vs. waiting for the FY25 annual report = a 15-month lead-time advantage.
*Limitation:* LinkedIn Sales Navigator licence cost (~Rs.7,000/month). Free LinkedIn search has rate-limit and visibility constraints. Naukri provides volume but lower data quality.

**11. Industry-association member directories and trade-fair exhibitor lists**
IDMA (Indian Drug Manufacturers Association), CHEMEXCIL, IDA (Indian Diagnostics Association), AMTZ, ABLE-India, IESA. Member directories are self-curated by domain. Trade-fair exhibitor lists from CPHI India, India Chem, Medical Fair India, and Bangalore India Bio identify companies that have committed marketing budget to growth — exhibition fees range from Rs.2L to Rs.20L per show.
*Why this is high-signal:* Both signals require active capital outlay and commitment to a specific segment. Self-classification reduces segment-misclassification errors.
*Limitation:* Some directories are pay-walled. Archival exhibitor lists are scattered and require manual aggregation.

---

## Tier 5 — Trade-flow signal (use with compliance caveat)

**12. Customs / import-export trade data**
Specialized commercial trade-data aggregators (Zauba, ExportGenius, Volza) compile shipping-bill data showing specific HS-code imports by Indian manufacturer. A company importing high-value specialty raw materials, proprietary intermediates, or specialty-grade reagents is by definition operating at specialty grade.
*Why this is high-signal:* Import bills cannot be faked. They prove *actual* operational scale, not marketing claim. Triangulating import patterns reveals process complexity that company websites obscure.
*Limitation and caveat:* Subject to compliance review under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP) 2023 for commercial use of trade-flow data referencing specific corporate entities. Aggregate sector-level use is uncontroversial; named-company prospecting may require additional review. Recommend treating this as a *verification* layer applied to candidates surfaced by methods 1–11, rather than as primary discovery — minimizes compliance risk while retaining most of the signal.

---

## How these 12 methods combine — the triangulation

The Federer profile emerges most cleanly from **triangulation across Land + Labor + Capital signals**, not from any single source:

- A company in MIDC Mega Park (Land) + hiring an R&D Lead on LinkedIn (Labor) + USFDA-approved facility (Capital) — is a **near-certain Federer**.
- A company on DSIR registry alone — is *probably* a Federer but needs verification.
- A company on an MIDC directory alone — is *possibly* a manufacturer; segment and scale unverified.

Standard candidate sourcing relies on Tier 1 alone (~50% precision). Adding Tier 2 (Land) and Tier 4 (Labor) raises precision substantially. Adding Tier 3 (Capital) and Tier 5 (Trade) materially improves precision for borderline cases — which is where most ICP scoring time is spent.

---

*Methods listed in order of recommended sequence — Tiers 1–2 build the universe, Tier 3 enriches, Tier 4 confirms growth-mode, Tier 5 verifies operational reality.*
