# Methodology

## Objective

Identify 25 companies operating in **Pune** (operational presence — HQ, R&D,
or primary manufacturing in the Pune district per the brief's Section 2 rule)
across two Basket A segments — *Custom synthesis & specialty chemicals* and
*Specialty diagnostics & life-science tools* — that match the **Federer
profile**:

- **Revenue band:** ₹50Cr–₹500Cr (per brief). Sub-floor candidates
  permitted only with explicit scale caveat following the Lazuline pattern
  in the brief's worked examples.
- **Ownership:** Founder/promoter-driven. Auto-disqualify subsidiaries of
  large groups, recently-acquired companies, and PE/VC-controlled entities.
- **Differentiation:** Patents, DSIR/USFDA/WHO-GMP recognition, or
  proprietary technical products. Auto-disqualify traders, distributors,
  CROs, testing labs, and bulk-commodity manufacturers.
- **Decision-maker:** Technical/scientific founder or MD (PhD, IIT/NIT/IISc,
  ex-ISRO/DRDO, scientist or engineer with publications).
- **Growth signals:** ≥2 of the brief's 5 thresholds (hiring 5+, facility
  expansion, new certification, current website, revenue growth).

# 🏙️ City & Segment Selection — Methodology

> **Decision:** Pune | Basket A — *Custom synthesis & specialty chemicals* + *Specialty diagnostics & life-science tools*

---

## 📌 How This Decision Was Made — The Reasoning Chain

> City and segment selection is the **single highest-leverage decision** in this assignment.
> A wrong city means 5 days of research that yields 12 passes instead of 25.
> This choice was made using a **structured elimination process — not intuition.**

---

Before picking any city, a minimum viable yield was calculated backward from the assignment brief.

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| **Required passes** | 25 |
| **Expected yield rate** | ~30% |
| **Companies to investigate** | ~75–100 |
| **Minimum sourcing universe needed** | **120–150 unique candidates** |

> 🔍 **Primary density tool:** DSIR R&D Recognition List (Table 1, 2,200+ entries)
> — the only publicly available government database pre-filtered to pharma/chem/diagnostics.
> Used as a **proxy for verified manufacturer density per city.**

### 🟡 Vadodara / Ahmedabad — Genuine Contender, Eliminated on Verification Cost

Gujarat has **11,000+ chemical plants** and accounts for **46–53% of India's chemical exports.**
GIDC clusters (Vapi, Ankleshwar, Dahej, Panoli) are the densest promoter-driven mid-cap
specialty-chem ecosystem in India. **Raw universe > Pune.**

**Why it was still eliminated:**

| Factor | Gujarat | Pune |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-company verification cost** | ~2× | Baseline |
| **Primary info sources** | Gujarati-language media + GIDC (manual) | BSE/NSE filings + English press |
| **Revenue band verification speed** | Slow | Fast |
| **Companies to research in 5 days** | 75–100 | 75–100 |

> 🎯 **Decisive factor:** At 2× verification cost per company and 75–100 companies to cover
> in a 5-day window, the time penalty made Gujarat unviable — despite its superior raw universe.

## 🏆 Why Pune Beats Vadodara — The Close Second

> Vadodara/Ahmedabad has the **highest raw yield** — Gujarat is the heartland of Indian
> specialty chemicals, and GIDC clusters are denser with promoter-driven mid-caps than
> anywhere else in India. **On pure yield, Gujarat wins.**
>
> But **three factors tip the balance to Pune** for a 5-day BBA-student-led research sprint:

### The Three Tipping Factors

| # | Factor | Pune Advantage | Gujarat Weakness |
|:---:|---|---|---|
| **i** | **Anchor case (Mylab)** | Textbook Federer — ICMR/CDSCO-approved, scientist-founder, in-band revenue, exports, post-COVID growth. **Easy to verify, instantly recognisable.** | No equivalent name-brand anchor. Every company needs a full explanation from scratch. |
| **ii** | **Verification ecosystem** | Heavy English-media coverage — ET, Mint, Moneycontrol, BSE/NSE filings. **Fact-check any claim in minutes.** | Company info sits behind GIDC databases or Gujarati-medium press. Per-company verification ≈ 2× longer. |
| **iii** | **Domain accessibility** | Pharma intermediates + agrochem intermediates — differentiation argument is **reachable for a finance student.** Named end-use applications make C3 scoring achievable. | Deep performance-chem niche. "Is N-methyl-2-pyrrolidone a commodity or specialty?" needs chemistry training or 30+ mins/company on Google Scholar. |

### Head-to-Head Summary

| Criterion | Vadodara / Ahmedabad | **Pune** | Winner |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Raw universe size | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Gujarat |
| Verification speed | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | **Pune** |
| Anchor case quality | ⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | **Pune** |
| Domain learnability (BBA) | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | **Pune** |
| CRO/trap exposure | Low 🟢 | Low 🟢 | Tie |
| 5-day feasibility | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | **Pune** |
| **Overall** | Strong on paper | **Strong in execution** | 🏆 **Pune** |

> **Bottom line:** Gujarat wins the yield war on paper.
> Pune wins the **execution war** in 5 days with a BBA-student skill set.
> The assignment rewards a well-verified shortlist of 25 — not the largest possible raw universe.

## Research Process

1. Pulled raw lists from each source (see `sources/`)
2. Applied geography + sector filter to reduce to ~150 candidates
3. Manual web/LinkedIn check to verify employee count and export activity
4. Removed companies failing any hard criterion (logged in `research-log/`)
5. Final 25 selected based on research gap + data availability for outreach

---

## Key Learnings

- MIDC directory is the richest source for mid-size chemical manufacturers but has stale contact data
- BSE SME filings give reliable revenue/employee data but many listed firms are too large (>500 employees)
- USFDA registration is the best proxy for export orientation in pharma
- Several promising DSIR-listed firms turned out to be subsidiaries of large groups and were disqualified
